Privacy Policy

Runestone Rush · Last updated June 25, 2026

Runestone Rush (“the app”, “we”, “us”) is a single-player puzzle game published by Danny Chen. This policy explains what information the app handles and why. We aim to collect as little as possible.

Information we collect

We do not ask you to create an account, and we do not collect your name, email address, or contacts. The app itself does not gather personal information about you.

Two categories of data are handled:

  • Game progress (stored only on your device). Your level progress and settings are saved locally on your device so you can continue where you left off. This data is not transmitted to us and is removed if you delete the app.
  • Advertising data (handled by Google AdMob). The app shows ads using Google AdMob. To serve and measure ads, Google may collect information such as device identifiers, approximate (non-precise) location derived from IP address, and ad interaction data. We do not receive this information in a form that identifies you.

Advertising

The app displays banner and occasional full-screen (interstitial) ads through Google AdMob. Runestone Rush is a general-audience app and requests non-personalized ads, which are based on contextual information (such as the current content) rather than a profile of your past behavior. Google may still use device identifiers for frequency capping, fraud prevention, and aggregated reporting.

Google’s handling of advertising data is described in How Google uses information from sites or apps that use our services and the Google Privacy Policy.

Consent (EEA, UK, and Switzerland)

Where required by law, the app uses Google’s User Messaging Platform to ask for your consent before personalized advertising or related data use. You can change your choice at any time from within the app where that option is offered.

Children’s privacy

The app is rated for general audiences and is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Ad requests are configured for non-personalized delivery.

Data retention & your choices

Local game data stays on your device until you delete the app. You can reset advertising identifiers using your device’s privacy settings (Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking / Apple Advertising on iOS), and you can review tracking permissions there at any time.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected here with a new “last updated” date.
